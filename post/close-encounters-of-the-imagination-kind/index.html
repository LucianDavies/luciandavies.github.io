Close encounters of the imagination kind

June 10, 2020

The story unfolds when I was much younger, definitely in the middle of primary school. Mother was working late and it was just Simba younger brother, Lingling younger sister and the house help who we shall call aunty. We were playing this game called deya and in English it translates to the singular of the space inside a curved leg. Don’t judge me these are kids games and the names reflect this.

The game is played with a ball and the object is to kick the ball through someone’s legs and on success everyone beats up the said individual until you do some pre-agreed action and then the game resets. In this case the action was to make you way from the sitting room through a hallway and proceed to touch the door to the main bedroom. Mother was not there so it was game on. During the course of the game to not get beaten to a pulp the players would do anything to minimize the chances of others landing successful blows and or tripping you sometimes we would hurl ourselves towards the door to a loud thunderous bang. Aunty was sick and tired from telling us to stop and after several iterations, we were getting tired then it happened.

A loud sound echoed through the house and the hairs on my back instantly sprung up. It sounded like shattered glass a window maybe. Aunty came to enquire what was going on only to find 3 children frozen in fear. No one was hurt and everyone was accounted for but what made the sound? We inched towards the guest bathroom first door on your left in the hallway. The air was tense but all for us hunched up together felt kinda safe. I reached for the door and slowly opened it. A slow hissing sound came from just behind the door and instantly we realised our mistake in that we had not switched in the bathroom light. Our survival instincts kicked in and all four of us bolted towards the main house door everything was a blur and before I realised it we were half breathing the ice-cold air outside.

The world was spinning, my heart beating a rhythm so loud it was on par with my brothers and cousins screams. I couldn’t think and after what seemed like an eternity I was snapped back to reality by a voice I did not recognize. Over the Durawall a phrase we use for the walls that separate house was a head. The person on the wall asked why we were screaming and before I could come to the conclusion that this head was real the others blurted out that something is in the house. The head disappeared and then after a couple of seconds the head appeared again attached to a body. The strange man from next door had what looked like a pocket knife in his mouth and a flashlight.

Our hero went around the garage and approached the back of the house. He asked some questions, but I was too gripped with suspense to answer. He said the window is okay and there is no broken glass then he casually walked back to the wall jumped on it and vanished to the other side. So now we have all calmed down, coherent thoughts and conversation now flowing. The question on everyone’s mind was if nothing broke into the house then what made the loud sound we all clearly heard.

We couldn’t stay outside any longer. So we entered the house everyone moving slowly enough so that they don’t lead the pack but not slow enough to be left to far behind. First room is the kitchen and we promptly armed ourselves. With our knives and wooden cooking spoons we slowly proceeded through the dinning room and left into the hallway. Our target the first door on the right in the hallway came up fast. This time we made sure to switch on the light. We inched in all of us compact into one unit always glancing behind to make sure that the others don’t desert you. I open the door slowly and the picked inside. A sense of confusion then relief washed over me it had turned out that bathroom mirror had fallen and shattering into many small pieces. The small peaces lodged themselves behind the door and when opened it made a screeching. This combined by our wild imagination made it sound like a hiss. We speculated days after that every time we banged the door to my mother’s bedroom it caused the mirror to shake and eventually fall.

We never did know the name of the person who came to our rescue I didn’t even check with the others if he was real, but we survived this terrible Friday.
